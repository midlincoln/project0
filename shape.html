Coming Back to Shape
and staying in shape

This part of the website is dedicated wrestlers who want to come back

Walking

Most difficult is to start doing something. Walking is easiest and provides the best results

  1. Walk with a dog if you have one
  2. Or walk to work if you don’t have a dog
  3. Start with once a day short walks and reach for 2 walks a day

The key is to be disciplined for 2 months or 3.

  1. The goals are is to start breathing better
  2. to feel some strength coming back to your legs
  3. use time while walking to strategise and to tackle various problems
Wrestling, Playing with a Dog

Once you have started walking, the next step is to get more physical action. I have a dog, so I try playing with it, wrestle with it and so on. What you can do with a dog

  1. to play football
  2. scramble with a dog
  3. sprint whoever is faster

The goal is to be acquire habits of playing with the dog so that

  1. you are in better mood
  2. you start feeling your muscles
  3. you sweat
Smoking less, Quitting

If you don’t smoke, you are lucky and can skip this part. But if you do, you are unlikely to just quit. While quitting could be a longer term goal you have to live with it for a while

  1. What helps to smoke less, is to go for long walks without cigarettes
  2. is to work in the environment where smoking is prohibited and you need to go outside to smock
  3. Quitting is always a goal, try as many times as you can

There were periods when me and my brother both smoked while we wrestled. But as pressure of the season increased we paused with the habit, returning to it, when the pressure is off. Sanasar smoked until 50, while i quit when was 30 and unfortunately started again at 46 then quit at 48 and started again at 49

  1. i admire folks who can smoke a cigarette a week and don’t fall into a habit..
  2. Once you quit, don’t smoke at all. No cigars, nothing
  3. Substitute smoking with light alcohol at initial stages of quitting
Drinking Less

Rehydrating on beer after good practice is a pleasure while with age, this puts more pressure on your systems and is not welcomed.

  1. Try to drink only good wine or beer
  2. try to drink good beer or wine only after good practice and not in other settings
  3. learn to cook good food, to be accompanied with good wine
  4. Decrease the amounts drank and go to sleep quickly, you will fill much better the next morning

If you are at a point in life when alcohol is a habit, seek further help and support. But if its still manageable and provides some positive feedback, then just try to minimize that

  1. Increasing intensity of workouts will help you to get enough body strength and shape to sustain alcohol damage
  2. do not ever drink hard liquor
  3. be open with your family and friends about your state of alcohol dependence
Starting Jogging

Following 2 or months of walking, you can start jogging

  1. keep walking, but do jog occasionally twice or 3 times a week
  2. or add some jogging while one your walks, run parts of your walking distance. Dog will help here
  3. Every day jogging is great, but do give yourself some rest skip days or weeks or even months, but keep walking during pauses from jogging
  4. Aim at jogging longer distance

Not everybody enjoy jogging, if you don’t then skip this step, but do keep walking. That’s essential, but do try jogging a longer runs especially if you are already in better shape. This is the easiest way to get pressure on our body

  1. run in the woods, or on river banks
  2. run slowly
  3. keep checking your blood pressure and over asses your changes in shape to decide wither to continue with jogging
Starting Wrestling Warm Ups

If you have access to wrestling gym, that’s great. Nothing is better than feel again the springing mat under your wrestling shoes (buy a pair)

  1. run and do warm-ups
  2. stretch yourself
  3. add more gymnastics
  4. do imaginary sweeps sprawls and throws
  5. do leg attacks
  6. practice imaginary gut ranch defence

If you don’t have access to the mat, try to get access to some gym

  1. do find a gym where it feels good
  2. some university gym or some suburb club
  3. do your research, connect with people, old friends, check out what they do, where they go to practice
Wrestling Practice 2 Times a Week, Sweating

Once you got access to the mat, try working out once or twice a week

  1. don’t ever go hard in the first couple of months, just play
  2. skip parts of the warming up, especially if you are in practice with yonger crowd
  3. do more of the drills, but also do some playing scramble and wrestling
  4. try your old moves but also try to learn new stuff, there is plenty of video support these days
  5. imitate different techniques, try John Smith shots, or try Sanasar’s smooth moves and tip toes stand. Explore

Spend another 5-12 months in this mode of very gentle practice.

  1. do go for some matches if there is an option
  2. but keep it cool, don’t go 100%
  3. protect your breath and heart, don’t get too tired
Adding Weekly Grappling or MMA Workout

Add some new style of wrestling, grappling, judo, mma or bjj, capoeira there are plenty of those these days. Get yourself a Gi

  1. start once a month
  2. and increase to once a week
  3. get new friends

Try participating in tournaments

  1. be very careful with your knees and ankles perhaps wear protection if unsure
  2. travel to different countries for tournaments. New styles have lots of tournaments around the world, and most anybody can participate
  3. do more than one style
Getting Ready for a First Tournament

If you decide to stage your comeback in your main Olympic style was it freestyle, Greco or judo, that’s fine. But otherwise do it in your new style

  1. try not to aim at veterans tournaments, go for real stuff, to wrestle with young and performing wrestlers
  2. you are likely get injured in your first tournament, but try to be prepared for it, and don’t give up, use plenty of ice initially
  3. come back to practice as soon as you can
